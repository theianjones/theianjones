Free Writing

Writing prompt: How do you archive your life?

  • How do you archive your life? #Tool for Thought
    • I archive my life by keeping track of my notes. I use a system called dendron.
    • It lets me capture my notes in a hierarchical manner. I like it because I don't have the friction of having to know where to store something.
    • It gives me tools for working with the hierarchies that I set up.
    • Since I am a developer i have to keep track of a lot of technologies., it gets overwhelming trying to manage all of my tabs when Im researching something.
    • So if i take the time to write what I find useful down, then I can actually come back to it and find it later. And when I do come back to it, it reinforces the usefulness of it in the first place.
  • This is a weird way to write. I feel like im running away from something. Or the timer is going to catch me and Im going to lose everything that I've gotten so far. This is an interesting way to provoke thoughts. #meta
  • Back to archiving my life. There is the concept of a digital-garden. It came from my brother/mentor Joel.
    • He wrote a good piece on it. Your blog is not a blog its a garden. You can nurture ideas over time. You start with a seedling of an idea that you think might have some roots and over time, you can see if you come back to it to edit it.
    • We've applied this at our work and do "mob gardening" sessions on the documents we use to plan our work. I like this idea because theres really no idea of "done".
    • Everything is a work in progress. You're goal is to create terms.evergreen-notes. Notes that will stand up to the test of time or will be applicable no matter what day of age it is. I think this use useful because it guards against trends you might fall into.
